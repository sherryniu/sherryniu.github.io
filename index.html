Bio

I am currently a postdoc in MEMS at WashU. Before working at WashU, I received the M.S. degree from University of Florida in May 2015, and the Ph.D. degree from The Ohio State University in Dec. 2018, both in materials science and engineering. I received my bachelor degree in polymer science and engineering from Beijing University of Chemical Technology in May 2013.

My research focuses on the development of functional biomaterials with biomedical applications. My studies include regenerative medicine methods using stimuli-responsive hydrogelsh, stem cells, and therapeutic peptides and growth factors. I'm interested in developing effective drug delivery stystems and testing their therapeutic potential in preclinical models of different diseases.

Research

Designing multifunctional polymers

Previously, I've developed multiple series of translational biomaterials relevant for therapeutic applications. The properties include but are not limited to biodegradable, bioimagable, oxygen-permeable, injectable, thermosensitive and ROS tunable. I'm interested in developing naturally derived polymers with desired properties for translational research.


Modulating cell behaviors with molecule cues

I'm also interested in modulating stem cell and the host cell behaviors to control cell proliferation, migration, and differentiation. By tuning the properties of developed materials, we can regulate the cell behaviors in patterns.


Controlling delivery of drugs

The developed polymers are particularly used as drug carriers for therapeutic molecules. The ultimate target is to maximize therapeutic effects.
